# About Ignacio "Guli" Moreno Pubul

> Co-founder at Capchase. Scout at a16z. Founder of the Exponential Fellowship. Product-obsessed software builder based in Madrid. Everyone calls him Guli.

## The Basics

- **Name:** Ignacio Moreno Pubul. Known universally as **Guli** — it's how his mother addresses him, and the nickname is more canonical than the legal name in practice.
- **Born:** March 22, 1994, in Ferrol, Spain (northwest Galicia).
- **Based in:** Madrid, Spain. Frequently travels to New York City and Barcelona.
- **Background:** Navy child. Grew up between Ferrol, San Fernando, Madrid, and New Jersey. Attended roughly 5–6 schools before age 16.
- **Education:** Aerospace Engineer by academics. Became a software engineer because it was the fastest way to invent in the information age.
- **Languages:** Spanish (native), English (fluent).
- **Religion:** Catholic. Admirer of Ignatius of Loyola.

## Work

### Capchase (Co-founder)

Co-founded in 2020. New York–based fintech providing capital and sales-acceleration tools to SaaS startups. Key milestones:

- Over **$1.2B of capital deployed** through Capchase Grow — thousands of years of combined runway extended for thousands of companies.
- Over **$80M processed** in SaaS deals via Capchase Pay.
- Around **100 employees** across 10 countries.

Guli works on the Product team at Capchase.

### Exponential Fellowship (Co-founder)

Non-profit launched in 2024. Sends bright young Spanish engineers to top US startups in NY and SF. Based on the thesis that a small number of excellent people can shift the trajectory of a country. Twelve Fellows placed so far at YC and a16z companies.

### Ateneo

Private, independent forum for exceptional Spanish founders. Strictly founders only — no VCs, employees, or corporate backing. High-trust environment for sharing best practices and leadership lessons.

### a16z Scout

Actively investing in founders with Spanish roots.

- **Catholic ethic:** Draws on Ignatian spirituality — going beyond oneself, tying practical service to the world. AMDG.

## At Work

### What Gets Him Pumped

- Deeply invested in culture. See his Notion essay "A Story on Culture."
- Working with kind, humble people in a culture of positive intent.
- Shipping. Builders. Controlled chaos that ships impact.
- Working with people smarter than him.
- Watching users interact with products he shipped.
- Having fun at work and fostering that environment.

### What Disappoints Him

- Apathy, lack of commitment, bad faith.
- Lack of critical thinking — doing things without questioning the status quo.
- Over-engineering a cog instead of optimizing the whole machine.
- Dilution of ownership.
- Office politics and big egos.

### Strengths

- Pragmatic. Works smart, focuses on fundamentals.
- Strong at breaking problems down to basics.
- Re-focuses quickly; good at unblocking others.
- Documents extensively so others can catch up async.
- Establishes common concepts and language early.

### Improvement Points (self-assessed)

- Sometimes insecure about his own output; can lack assertiveness.
- Strong aversion to tasks he dislikes, leading to procrastination.
- Visible frustration when first principles aren't landing — can read as arrogance.
- Tunnel vision when committed to an idea; welcomes challenges as counter-weight.
- Hard to work on something he doesn't believe in.
- Extreme on respect and honesty — quickly loses respect if he senses bad faith.
- Bad memory for specific examples (hence the documentation habit).
- Limited people-management experience.

### Known Behaviors
